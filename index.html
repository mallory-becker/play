03 — Beliefs

A short list of things I keep coming back to.

Not a manifesto. Just the rules of thumb I make decisions against.
On AI

AI is a thinking partner, not a shortcut.

I use AI daily as a thinking tool — for drafts, synthesis, and breaking patterns — but always with a critical eye. It accelerates what I already understand. Good judgment, strong character, and human intelligence still do the best work.

use it · don't outsource to it
On craft

Work hard and work thoroughly.

The second draft is where it gets good; the fifth is where it gets right. I'd rather ship one thing I can be proud of than five things I'll have to apologize for later. Thoroughness compounds — in research, in pixels, in the questions you ask before the meeting.

measure twice · cut once · then check it
On the user

Care about the person on the other side of the screen.

Someone is going to use this on a Tuesday at 4:47pm, late on a deadline, with a bad headset and a worse manager. My job is to lower their heart rate, not raise it. Every flow gets the same question: would a real person, having a real day, feel respected by this?

design for the bad day, not the demo
On the whole life

Do my best in all areas of life.

The same standards I bring to a design review I try to bring to a long run, a friendship, a difficult conversation, a Sunday dinner. Design is a practice, and the practice doesn't stop when I close the laptop. The best designers I know are good neighbors first.

whole person · whole work
