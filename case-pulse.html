A music player
for people who
hate scrubbing.

Pulse is an audio app built around a single, opinionated idea: scrubbing through a track shouldn't feel like dragging a wet napkin across glass. We rebuilt the seek bar from the spine out — and reduced "missed seeks" by 71% in the process.

01 — The problem

Every scrub is a small failure.

We pulled 30 days of session logs from the beta and found something embarrassing: 62% of seeks were corrected within 4 seconds. Users were scrubbing, missing, scrubbing again. Each "missed" seek is a tiny moment of frustration that compounds.

The default iOS seek bar is a 4-pixel-tall line with a 12-pixel thumb. On a moving train, with a finger the size of a finger, it's hopeless. Existing solutions either made the bar bigger (ugly) or added a slow-down zoom (slow). Neither matched the way people actually listen.

"If a thousand users keep doing the wrong thing, the user is not the problem."

02 — The bet

What if the bar knew about the music?

Every track has natural anchors: the drop, the chorus, the bridge, the moment the strings come in. People don't seek to 1:42; they seek to "the part where it gets good." So we proposed something obvious in retrospect: detect beat markers from the waveform on the device, and make the thumb magnetic to them.

Engineering pushed back hard. Audio analysis on a mid-range iPhone, in real time, while playing back, while keeping the UI at 120fps. We agreed to a constraint: it had to feel free. No spinners, no progress UI for the analysis. If it took longer than 1.2 seconds after a track loaded, we'd ship without it on that song. Forgiving, by design.

03 — The motion vocabulary

Three easing curves do almost all the work in this app.

cubic-bezier(0.65, 0, 0.35, 1) · 450ms

04 — The work

Tighten, then tighten again.

We shipped seven prototype builds before settling. The first version made the thumb magnetic to every beat — turns out a track has 800 of them, and magnetism becomes mushy. The fifth version was the opposite: only chorus and bridge markers, with a tiny haptic tick on each. That felt great until the audio engineer pointed out that a 30-second ambient intro now had zero magnetism — you couldn't seek into it at all.

Version seven is what shipped: magnetism scales inversely with marker density. Sparse tracks get strong snaps; dense tracks get a gentle nudge. The bar always feels like it's pulling toward something — even on a 14-minute drone piece.

Smart default: if you don't know which beat to land on, the bar lands you on the next chorus. You can always override.

05 — Outcomes

The numbers, with the caveat
that all numbers lie a little.

Six weeks after launch, with ~140k installs and a stable cohort to measure against:

  • −71% "Missed seeks" — defined as a seek corrected within 4 seconds.
  • +34% Average session length, week 2 vs. week 6.
  • 4.8 App Store rating across 2,400 reviews. We're proud, but two reviews said it gave them motion sickness, which is fair.
  • 1 Apple Design Award nomination, Interaction category. Didn't win, learned plenty.
06 — What I'd do differently

The honest list.

We over-indexed on the scrub for the first two months and almost shipped without working AirPlay controls. Two reviewers gently pointed out that you can't scrub if the app's not playing music yet. Lesson: the hero interaction is not the whole app.

I also wish we'd built the accessibility story in from week one instead of week ten. VoiceOver scrub is genuinely good now, but it took an extra two sprints to retrofit, and there's a version of this story where we didn't have the budget to do it right. That version is the one I think about.
